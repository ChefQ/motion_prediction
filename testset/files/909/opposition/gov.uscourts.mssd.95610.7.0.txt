ORDER FINDING DEFENDANT COMPETENT TO STAND TRIAL

 

_ This cause having come before the Court for hearing on the issue of the Defendant's competence and
mental state at the time of the offense and the Court listened to all evidence offered by the Defendant as well
as considered the report of Dr. Chris Lott, expert appointed by the Court, and after considering all evidence
and argument, finds

(A) the Defendant has sufficient present ability to consult with his attorney with a reasonable
degree of rational understanding in the preparation of his defense, and has a rational as well
as factual understanding of the nature and object of the legal proceedings against him; and

(B) the Defendant would have been able to know the nature and quality of his alleged actions
and he would have been able to know the difference between right and wrong in relation to
his alleged actions at the time of the alleged offense.

ITIS, THEREFORE, ORDERED AND ADJUDGED, that the Court finds beyond areasonable doubt
that the Defendant is competent to stand trial, is able to assist counsel at trial, and was not McNaughton
insane at the time of the alleged offense.
